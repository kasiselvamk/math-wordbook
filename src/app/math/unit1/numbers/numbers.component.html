Natural Numbers And Whole Numbers

  1. Natural Numbers :

    Natural numbers are denoted by 'N'.numbers starting from '1 ' (one).

    N = {{ "{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, .........}" }}

  2. Whole Numbers :

    Whole numbers are denoted by 'W'.numbers starting from '0' (zero).

    W = {{ "{0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, .........}" }}

  3. Even Numbers :

    Numbers which are the multiples of '2' are called 'Even Numbers '.

    E = {{ "{2, 4, 6, 8, .........}" }}

  4. Odd Numbers :

    Numbers which are not the multiples of '2' are called 'Odd Numbers'.

    Odd = {{ "{1, 3, 5, 7, .........}" }}

  5. The smallest Natural Number is '1 ' and the smallest Whole Number is '0'.

  6. There do not exist the largest Natural number and the largest Whole number.

  7. The successor of any whole number is one more than that whole number.

    Ex : Successor of 11 is 12, (11 + 1)

  8. The predecessor of any whole number is one less than that whole number.

    Ex : Predecessor of 11 is 10 (11 - 1)

  9. There is no predecessor of zero in whole numbers.

  10. Face value and place value :

    • Face value of a digit is the digit itsell

      Ex.: Face value of '5' in 325 is '5'

    • Place value of a digit is the value of the digit in its place.

      Ex. : Place value of '5' in 352 is 50 [5 \times 10]

  11. Consecutive Numbers :

    The Numbers which differ from each other by one are called 'Consecutive Numbers'.

    Ex. : 13, 14 and 19, 20 are consecutive numbers

Properties Of Natural Numbers and Whole Numbers

    Let a, b and c be any three Whole Numbers. Then

  1. Closure Property :

    • a + b is a whole number

      Ex: 3 + 4 = 7 is a whole number

    • a \times b is a whole number

      Ex: 3 \times 4 = 12 is a whole number

  2. Commutative Property :

    • a + b = b + a

      Ex: 4 + 5 = 5 + 4 = 9

    • a \times b = b \times a

      Ex: 4 \times 5 = 5 \times 4 = 20

  3. Associative Property :

    • a + (b + c) = (a + b) + c

      Ex: 3 + (4 + 5) = (3 + 4) + 5 = 12

    • a \times (b \times c) = (a \times b) \times c

      Ex: 3 \times (4 \times 5) = (3 \times 4) \times 5 = 60

  4. Distributive Property :

    • a \times (b + c) = (a \times b) + (a \times c)

      Ex: 3 \times (4 + 5) = (3 \times 4) + (3 \times 5) = 27

    • a \times (b - c) = (a \times b) - (a \times c)

      Ex: 3 \times (5 - 4) = (3 \times 5) - (3 \times 4) = 3

    • a + 0 = 0 + a = a
    • a - 0 = a
    • a \times 1 = 1 \times a = a
    • a + 1 = a
    • a - b \neq b - a
    • a \times 0 = 0 \times a = 0
    • 0 + a = 0 and {{"\\frac{a}{0}"}} is not defined.

Integers

  1. Integers :

    Integers are the collection of all the Natural numbers with both positive and negative sign along with zero.

    • Integers = {{ "{........., -4, -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 ,.........}" }}
    • Positive Integers = {{ "{1, 2, 3, 4 ,.........}" }}
    • Negative Integers = {{ "{........., -4, -3, -2, -1}" }}
    • Zero is neither positive nor negative.

  2. Absolute Value :

    The absolute value ofan integer is the numerical value (magnitude) irrespective of its sign.

    Ex: \lvert 6 \rvert=6\, and\, \lvert -6 \rvert=6

  3. Closure Property :

    If 'a' and 'b' are any two integers then a + b is also an integer.

    Ex: 4 + 5 = 9 \,and\, 4 + (-5) = -1

    • '0' is the identity element in addition.

      Ex: 5 + 0 = 5 \,and\, -5 + 0 = -5

    • '1' is the identity element in multiplication

      Ex: 5 \times 1 = 1 \times 5 = 5 \,and\, -5 \times 1 = -5

  5. Inverse :

    • The additive inverse of any integer 'a' is '-a'.

      Ex: The additive inverse of 4 is -4

    • The multiplicative inverse of any integer 'a' is {{"\\frac{1}{a}"}} \,\, (a \neq 0)

      Ex : The multiplicative inverse of 4 is {{"\\frac{1}{4}"}}

  6. Number line :

    Every whole number on the right of the number line is greater than every whole number to its left.

    Ex. 5 > 0; 5 > 1; 5 > -3; -3 > -4; 0 > -2

    • Every positive integer is greater than every negative integer.

    • Zero is less than every positive integer.

    • Zero is greater than every negative integer.

RATIONAL NUMBERS

  1. Rational Number :

    A number which can be expressed in the form {{"\\frac{p}{q}"}} where 'p' and 'q' are integers and \,(q \neq 0) is called a Rational number.

    Ex: {{"\\frac{3}{4}"}} {{"-\\frac{4}{7}"}}\,and\,{{"-\\frac{5}{8}"}}

  2. Rational Numbers satisfy the

    • (i) Closure property.
    • (ii) Commutative property.
    • (iii) Associative property.
    • (iv) Distributive property.

    as in the case of Natural numbers and Integers.
