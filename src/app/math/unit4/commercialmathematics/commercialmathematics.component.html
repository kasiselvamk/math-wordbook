RATIO AND PROPORTION

  1. Ratio : Comparison of two of two quantities of the same kind will result in a ratio.

    The ratio of a number 'a' to another number 'b' (b \neq 0)\;is\;the\;quotient {{"\\frac{a}{b}"}} and is written as a:b where 'a' and 'b' are called its terms.

    If should the two be quantities are in same the same unit(kind) they should be converted into the same unit.

    E.g. (i) 7 Boys, 9 Girls

    Ratio of Boys to girls is 7 : 9

    Ratio of Girls to boys is 9 : 7

    (ii)1 dozen to 1 score

    1 doz. = 12 items ; 1 score = 20 items

    therefore Ratio is 12:20 \begin{{"{bmatrix}"}} \frac{{"{12}{4}"}} & \frac{{"{20}{4}"}} \end{{"{bmatrix}"}} 3:5

    (iii) Note:3:4=6:8

    20:30=2:3

  2. Proportion : When two ratios are equal they are said to be in proportion.

    4:5 = 24:30

    4\times30=5\times24=120

    So, Product of means = Product of extremes in a proportion.

    4:K :: K:16

    K^2 = 4\times16=64

    K^2=8^2 \implies K=8

    K is called the mean proportional between 4 and 16.

  3. Percentage and its Applications :

    Percentage : A fraction with its denominator as 100 is called a percent.

    E.g. 9:100\;is\;9\% \;or\;{{"\\frac{9}{100}"}}=9\%

    (i) {{"\\frac{16}{25}"}}={{"\\frac{16}{25}"}}\times100=64\%

    (ii) 75\% = {{"\\frac{75}{100}"}}={{"\\frac{3}{4}"}}\;or\;3:4

  1. Cost Price and Selling Price :

    The money paid by the shopkeeper to buy the goods is called the Cost price (C.P.) and

    the price at which the goods are sold is called the Selling Price (S.P.).

    C.P. includes overhead charges also.

  2. Marked Price : The price that is mentioned on a commodity is called the Marked Price.

  3. Profit and Loss :

    If S.P > C.P., Profit = S.P. - C.P.

    If S.P < C.P., Loss = C.P. - S.P.

    Profit\%={{"\\frac{Profit}{C.P}"}}\times100

    Loss\%={{"\\frac{Loss}{C.P}"}}\times100

  4. S.P=C.P. + Gain or C.R - Loss

    S.P= {{"\\frac{100-Loss\\% }{100}"}} \times C.P\;or

    S.P= {{"\\frac{100-Gain\\% }{100}"}} \times C.P

  5. Simple Interest :

    Simple Interest S.I = {{"\\frac{PTR}{100}"}} Where P = Principal, T = Time, R = Rate of interest.

    Amount 'A' = P + S.I.

  6. Discount : Discount is offered on the Marked Price (M.P.) of the article by the shopkeeper and is generally expressed as a percentage of the M.P.

  7. Compound Interest :

    Amount A =P\left(1+{{"\\frac{r}{100}"}}\right)^n

    and C.I. = A - P or

    P \begin{{"{bmatrix}"}} \left( {{"1+\\frac{r}{100}"}} \right)^n -1 \end{{"{bmatrix}"}}

    Where P is the principal, r is the rate of interest and n is the number of times that interest being calculated.

  8. Growth and Depreciation :

    The increased quantity after certain growth (over a period of time) is found out by using the formula

    A= P\left(1+{{"\\frac{r}{100}"}}\right)^n

    In the case of depreciation, decay or decrease the rate r is replaced by (-r) in the formula

    A= P\left(1-{{"\\frac{r}{100}"}}\right)^n
