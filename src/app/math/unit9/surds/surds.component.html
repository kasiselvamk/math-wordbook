SURDS

  1. Definition :

    If 'a' is a positive rational number an irrational number of the type \sqrt[n]a or a^{{"\\frac{1}{n}"}} is called a Surd.

    The symbol \sqrt[n]{{"{}"}} is called the radical. Sign 'n' is called the order of the surd and 'a' is called the radicand.

    Eg : a) \sqrt 3 is a surd of order 2 and 3 is the radicand.

    b) \sqrt[5]7 is a surd of order 5 and 7 is the radicand.

    Note : \sqrt{{"{7+\\sqrt5}"}} is not a surd because is not a rational number.

    7 + \sqrt5

    \sqrt 5\;, \sqrt[5]4 are pure surds.

  4. Similar Surds or like Surds:

    5 \times\sqrt 7\;,9\times\sqrt 7 are like Surds. (Same irrational factors)

  5. Dissimilar surds or unlike surds :

    \sqrt 5\;,6\times\sqrt 11 are unlike Surds. (Different irrational factors).

  6. Addition and subraction of surds :

    2\sqrt 3 + 5\times\sqrt 13 = 7\times\sqrt 3

    4\sqrt 7 - \sqrt 7 = 3\times\sqrt 7

    Note : Only similar surds are involved.
