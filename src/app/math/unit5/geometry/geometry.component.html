POINTS AND LINES

  1. A point has a position and its location can be ascertained. It has no dimension and direction.

  2. Points are denoted by Capital letters as A, B, C, D.

  3. Line is straight and extends indefinitely in both directions.

  4. A line can be denoted by a single small letter or by

  5. using any two points A and B on it.

  6. A line has no end point.

  7. Only one line can be drawn passing through two given points.

  8. Many number of lines can be drawn through a given point.

  9. Collinear Points :-

    Three or more points are said to be collinear if all of them lie on the same straight line.

  10. Plane : A plane is a flat surface which extends indefinitely in all directions.

  11. Intersecting Lincs :

    if two distinct lines in a plane have one point in common they are called intersecting lines.

  12. The common point is called point of intersection.

  13. Non-intersecting lines :

    If two distinct lines in a plane have no point common they are called non-intersecting lines.

  14. Concurrent lines :

    Three or more distinct lines in a plane are said to be concurrent if they all pass through the same point.

  15. The common point is called point of concurrence.

  16. Line Segment : A line segment is a portion of a line. It has two end points. It is denoted by \overline{{"{xy}"}}

  17. Only one line segment can be drawn joining two given points.

  18. Ray : If a part of a line has one end point but extends indefinitely in the other direction then it is called a ray.

    O. Initial point

  19. Opposite rays : Two rays with same initial point but directed in the opposite direction.

    O - initial point ; OA, 0B - opposite rays.

  20. Any number ofrays can be drawn from a given initial point.

TRIANGLES

  1. Triangle : A three sided closed figure is called a triangle

    Sides : AB, BC, CA, Vertices : A, B, C Angles LA, LB, ZC (or) c ZCAB, ZABC, ZBCA

  2. Types of Triangles :

    Based on Sides;

    (i) Scalene triangle : All sides are unequal.

    (ii) Isosceles triangle : Two sides are equal.

    (iii) Equilateral triangle : All sides are equal.

    Based on angles :

    (iv) Acute Angled triangle : All the angles are acute.

    (v) Right angled triangle: One of the angles in a triangle is right angle.

    (vi) Obtuse angled triangle : One of the angles in a triangle is obtuse angle.

  3. The sum of the three angles of a triangle is 180^0.

  4. The sum of any two sides will be greater than the third side

  5. In a triangle, if two sides are equal then the angles opposite to them are also equal.

  6. Pythagoras theorem

    In a right triangle the square on the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares on other two sides.

    Eg : Pythagorean triplets.

    (3, 4, 5), (5, 12, 13) (7, 24, 25), (8, 15, 17)

  7. Median : A Median of a triangle is the line segment that joins a vertex to the middle point of the opposite side.

    (i) medians of a triangle are concurrent at the point G called the Centroid of the triangle.

    (ii) The Centroid of a triangle divides each median in the ratio 2 : 1.

  8. Altitude : An altitude of a triangle is the line segnent from a vertex of the triangle perpendicular to the opposite side.

    (i) The Altitudes of a triangle are concurrent at Orthocentre O.

  9. Perpendicular bisector : A line which bisects the given line segment at right angles is called the perpendicular bisector.

    (i) The perpendicular bisectors of the sides of a triangle are concurrent at circumcentret

    A ray which bisects the given angle is called angle bisector.

  11. In an equilateral triangle the Centroid, the orthocentre, the circumcentre and the incentre coincide.

  12. In a right triangle the orthocentre is at the vertex of the right angle.

  13. In a right triangle the circumcentre lies on the mid point of the hypotenuse.

  14. Congruent figures:

    Two figures are congruent if they have the same shape and size.

    Two plane figures are congruent if each, when superposed on the other covers it exactly.

    Two line segments are congruent, if they have the same length.

  15. angles are congruent if they have the same measure.

  16. For two triangles to be congruent.

    (i) SSS Congruence Condition : Three sides of one triangle are equal to the three sides of the other triangle.

    (ii)SAS Congruence Condition : Two sides and the included angle of the one are respectively equal to the two sides and the included angle of the other.

    (iii) AAS Congruence Condition : Two angles and one side of one are equal to two angles and the corresponding side of the other.

    (iv) RHS Congruence Condition : Two right triangles are congruent, if the hypotenuses are equal and one side of the one is equal to the corresponding side of the other.

  17. A line through the mid point of a side of a triangle parallel to another side bisects the third side.

    (ii) The line segment joining the mid points of two sides of a triangle c is parallel to the third side.

  18. If a line parallel to one side of a triangle intersects the other two sides in distinct points then it cuts off from these sides proportional to these sides
