MULTIPLES AND FACTORS

  1. Multiples:

    The numbers 4, 8, 12, 16, 20 .... are obtained by multiplying 4 by 1,2,3,4.... The numbers 4, 8, 12, 16, 20 .... are called multiples of 4.

    a number is a multiple of itself

    Eg :3 is a multiple of 3.

    Every number is a multiple of 1

    Eg :4 is a multiple of 1.

  2. Factors:

    A number is a factor of another number if the second number is divisible by the frst number.

    Eg : 28 is divisible by 7

    7 is a factor of 28.

    1 is a factor of every number. Every number is a factor of itself.

  3. Prime Number:

    A number that has exactly 2 factors is called a prime number.

    Eg : 2, 3, 5, 7 etc.

    2 is the only even prime number.

  4. Composite Number :

    A number that has more than 2 factors is called a composite number.

    Eg :4, 6, 8, 9,10 etc.

    1 is neither prime nor composite.

  5. Twin Primes :

    Twin Primes are those two prime numbers which differ by 2.

    Eg: 3 and 5; 5 and 7.

  6. Co-prime Numbers :

    TW0 numbers which have only 1 (ONE) as their common factor are called co-prime numbers.

    Eg: 9 and 16.

TEST FOR DIVISIBILITY OF NUMBERS

  1. Divisibility by 2 :

    A number is divisible by 2 if its unit digit is 0 or is an even number.

    Eg : 40, 26

  2. Divisibility by 3 :

    A number is divisible by 3 if the sum of its digits is divisible by 3.

    Eg : 63 is divisible by 3 since 6 + 3 = 9 is divisible by 3.

  3. Divisibility by 4 :

    A number is divisible by 4 if its last two digits together is divisible by 4.

    Eg : 648 is divisible by 4 since 48 is divisible by 4.

  4. Divisibility by 5 :

    A number is divisible by 5 if its last digit is either 0 or 5

    Eg : 310, 205

  5. Divisibility by 6 :

    A number is divisible by 6 if it is divisible by both 2 and 3

    Eg : 108 is divisible by 2

    1+0+8=9 is divisible by 3.Hence 108 is divisible by 6

  6. Divisibility by 8 :

    A number is divisible by 8 if 1ts last 3 digits together is divisible by 8

    Eg : 4168 -> 168 is divisible by 8

    Hence 4168 is divisible by 8.

  7. Divisibility by 9 :

    A number divisible by is 9 if this sum of its digits is divisible by 9

    Eg : 6183 is divisible by 9

    Since 6+1+8+3 =18 is divisible by 9

  8. Divisibility by 10 :

    A number is divisible by 10 if its last digit is zero.

    Eg : 30, 1400.

  9. Digisibility by 11 :

    A number is divisible by 11, if the difference between the sum of its digits in odd places and in even places is either 0 or a multiple of 11.

    Eg : 5 6 2 9 4 7

    6+9+7= 22

    5+2+4= 11

    22 - 11= 11

    11 is multiple of 11. Hence 562947 is divisible by 11

  10. Prime Factorisation :

    A factorisation is prime if all the factors are primes.

    Eg : Prime factorisation of 24 = 2 x 3x 2 x 2

L.C.M AND H.C.F

  1. L.C.M :

    If two numbers are co-primes, then L.C.M. is the product ofthe numbers.

    Eg : 2 and 3

    L.C.M.is 2 * 3 = 6

    If one number is the multiple of the other, the greater number is the L.C.M. of the numbers.

    Eg : 4 and 8

    L.C.M. is 8.

    The lowest common multiple of two or more numbers is the smallest number which is divisible by those numbers.

    Eg : Multiples of 6 = 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36.

    Multiples of 5 = 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 35.

    L.C.M of 5 and 6 is 30.

  2. H.C.F :

    H.C.F of two or more numbers is the product of all common prime factors.

    Eg : 12 = 2 * 2 * 3; 8 = 2 * 2 * 2

    H.C.F. of 12 and 8 is 2x2 = 4

EXPONENTS AND RADICALS

    a is written as a^1

    a \times a is written as a^2

    a \times a \times a is written as a^3

    In general a \times a \times a.......n times = a^n

    We say that `a` is the base and `n` is the exponent.Zero and Negative Exponents.

  2. For every non-zero real number 'a.'

    a^0 = 1

    345^0 = 1

    {{"\\frac{3}{8}"}}^0 = 1

  3. For every non-zero real number `a` and every positive integer `n`

    For all real numbers a and b and for all integers m and n.

  1. SQUARES AND SQUARE ROOTS

    A number which has exactly two equal factors is called a square number. Each factor is called the square root of the number.

    16 = 4\times4 \to 4 is the Square root of 16 and 16 is the square of 4

    To indicate square root we use the symbol \sqrt{{"{`}"}}

    Thus \sqrt{{"{49}"}} =7 ; \sqrt{{"{81}"}} =9

    1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36 etc. are the squares of the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, . etc. respectively. Such numbers are called perfect squares

    Square root by factorisation :

    Find \sqrt{{"{36}"}} ;

    36 = 2\times2 \times 3\times3 = 2^2 \times 3^2

    \sqrt{{"{36}"}} = 2\times3

    \sqrt{{"{36}"}} = 6
