LOCI AND CONCURRENCY

  1. The locus of a point is the set of all points which satisfy some given condition(s). The plural of locus is LOCI.

  2. The locus of a point equidistant form two given points is the perpendicular bisector of the line segment joining the two points [Figure : 1.1]

  3. The locus of a point equidistant form two intersecting lines is the pair of lines bisecting the angles formed by the given lines. [Figure 1.2]

  4. AREA :

    Parallelograms on equal bases and between same parallels are equal in area. Triangles on the same base and between the same parallels are equal in area. If a triangle and parallelogram are on the same base and between the same parallels, the area of the triangle is equal to half that of the parallelogram.

  5. SIMILAR TRIANGLES :

    Two triangles are said to be similar to each other if

    (a) their corresponding angles are equal - and

    (b) the lengths of their corresponding sides are proportional

    Two polygons are said to be similar if

    (a) their corresponding angles are equal and

    (b) the lengths of their corresponding sides are proportional.

  6. AAA Similarity :

    If in two triangles corresponding angles are equal then the triangles are similar.

  7. SSS Similarity :

    If the corresponding sides of two triangles are proportional then the triangles are similar.

  8. AA Corollary :

    If two angles of one triangle are respectively gqual to two angles of another triangle, then the two triangles are similar.

  9. SAS Similarity :

    If in two triangles, one pair of corresponding sides are proportional and the included angles are equal then the two triangles are similar.
